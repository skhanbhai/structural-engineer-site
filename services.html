Buyer questions FAQ

Questions clients ask before commissioning a service.

If yours isn’t here, send a short brief through contact — we read every enquiry.

Do you cover all of London and the South East?

Yes. Most residential work is in Greater London — Fulham, Hammersmith, Wandsworth, Lambeth, Southwark, Camden, Islington, Hackney, Haringey, Brent, Ealing, Richmond, Kingston and surrounding boroughs — with regular projects in the commuter belt across the South East.

Can you do both the architectural drawings and the structural calculations?

Yes. Architectural design and structural engineering are coordinated in-house, so the drawings, calculations and specification align from the outset. If you already have an architect, we work alongside them and produce structural calculations and drawings that match their package.

See Architectural Design (S/02) and Structural Engineering (S/01).

Do you handle planning and building control submissions, or do I?

Either. We can prepare drawings to a standard the local authority and building control body accept, and manage the submission, responses to officer queries and conditions on your behalf where useful. Smaller schemes are often submitted directly by the homeowner using the drawings we issue.

See Planning Support (S/03) for typical timelines.

Are calculations and reports signed by a chartered structural engineer?

Yes. Structural calculations, structural drawings and inspection reports are signed and stamped by a chartered structural engineer (IStructE) and prepared in formats accepted by building control bodies, solicitors, insurers and mortgage lenders.

How are fees structured?

Most residential engagements are fixed-fee, quoted in writing once scope is clear — typically within 48 hours of receiving project details. Larger or staged projects can be split into stage fees (concept, planning, technical design, construction support) so payment tracks deliverables.

Can you advise before I commit to anything?

Yes. Most enquiries start with a short call or photo-and-postcode message. We’ll tell you honestly what the project sensibly needs — structural calcs only, a full design package, a permitted development route, or just a survey — before any fee is committed.

Send a brief via contact or use the photo-first crack inspection route for cracking concerns specifically.

Do you provide site supervision or build-stage support?

Yes — site visits, RFIs, design clarifications and snagging-stage queries can be included as a build-stage retainer or charged ad-hoc. This is the most common reason a contractor recommends us in the first place: drawings that are clear on site, and an engineer who answers the phone when something needs a quick decision.

Worried about cracks specifically?

The crack inspection page covers it in detail: photo-first triage, when to call an engineer, common causes, fees and turnaround. Most enquiries don’t need a full report — we’ll tell you if yours doesn’t.
