Section / 03 Straight answers

The short answers, before you check.

These are the honest summaries behind the checker's results. Your project's specifics can change the picture, which is what the 60-second checker is for.

Do I need a structural engineer to remove a load-bearing wall?

Yes, in almost all cases. Removing or altering a load-bearing wall normally requires Building Regulations approval, and Building Control will generally expect structural calculations showing the new beam — usually an RSJ or steel — has been sized correctly, with the supports at each end checked. Your builder installs the beam; the design behind it is the engineer’s job.

RSJ & steel beam calculations → Check your project →

Do I need a structural engineer for chimney breast removal?

Yes. The masonry above a removed chimney breast, including any remaining breast and the stack, has to be properly supported. Building Control will generally expect that support — steel beams or, where accepted, gallows brackets — to be designed and justified with calculations before signing the work off.

Chimney breast removal → Check your project →

Do I need a structural engineer for an extension?

Yes, alongside your architect. Extensions change how loads travel through the building: new beams, lintels, foundations and altered load paths normally need structural calculations for Building Control, usually working from your architect’s drawings. Opening the rear wall for bifolds typically means a steel, sometimes a goalpost frame.

Extension structural engineering → Check your project →

Do I need a structural engineer for a loft conversion?

Yes, for a conversion to a usable room. A loft conversion means a new floor structure, usually new steels, and often roof alterations — a dormer adds more. All of that normally needs calculations for Building Control approval. Boarding the loft for genuinely light storage is a different story: see below.

Loft conversions → Check your project →

Do I need a structural engineer for cracks in my house?

Not always. Most hairline cracks are cosmetic: seasonal movement or plaster shrinkage rather than a structural problem. Cracks wide enough to fit a coin in, cracks that keep reopening or growing, or doors and windows starting to stick are the ones worth a proper look — photos are usually enough to start.

Crack inspection London → Check your cracks →

When might I not need a structural engineer?

Genuinely non-load-bearing stud partitions, light loft boarding for storage, and most hairline cracks usually don’t need one. The catch is the word “genuinely”: walls that look like stud partitions sometimes carry floor joists, purlin struts or the wall above. If there’s any doubt, a few photos gets you an honest answer either way.

Section / 04 FAQ

Checker FAQs.

If yours is not here, send it via the form or on WhatsApp. We read every enquiry.

Is the checker’s result structural advice?

No. The result is indicative guidance based on your answers: a well-informed starting point, not structural advice and not a substitute for an engineer’s assessment or calculations for your specific property. Buildings vary, and the checker cannot see yours. If your project involves removing or altering structure, the result will point you to the right next step.

When do I definitely need a structural engineer?

Whenever the work removes or alters something that holds part of the building up: load-bearing walls, chimney breasts, openings in external walls, loft conversions, extensions. In those cases a beam or support has to be sized with calculations, and Building Control will normally want to see them before signing the work off.

When might I not need one?

Genuinely non-load-bearing stud partitions, light loft boarding for storage, and most hairline cracks usually don’t need an engineer. The catch is the word “genuinely”: walls that look like stud partitions sometimes carry load. If there is any doubt, photos are usually enough for us to give you a quick, honest view.

What happens to my answers?

Nothing, unless you choose to send them. Your result appears on screen with no sign-up or contact details required. If you then tap “Get a quote” or the WhatsApp button, your answers are summarised into the enquiry so you don’t have to repeat yourself. That’s the only time they leave your browser.

Can I share my result?

Yes. When your result appears, the page address updates to include it. Copy the link from your browser and anyone who opens it will see the same result. Handy for sharing with a partner or your builder.

Do you cover my area?

We help homeowners, builders and property professionals across South West London (Putney, Wandsworth, Fulham, Wimbledon, Richmond, Hammersmith) and the wider London area where suitable. Send your postcode with your enquiry and we’ll confirm.
