Section / 01 When calculations help

What your side-return needs calculations for.

A side-return infill concentrates a lot of structure into a narrow strip. These are the elements Building Control will expect to see justified.

Opening the side wall

The flank wall of the back addition carries floors and roof above. Steels span the return and pick it up, so the kitchen below stays open from wall to wall.

Opening the rear wall too

Most side-returns open the rear at the same time for bifolds or sliders. Two walls coming out together changes how the whole back of the house is held stable.

Box frames and goalpost frames

When side and rear open together, the corner often needs a box or goalpost frame: steel beams and columns designed as one system to keep the structure stable.

Victorian and Edwardian terraces

The classic side-return house. Old brickwork, shallow footings and party walls each side mean the existing structure needs checking, not just the new steel.

Party wall considerations

The side return usually sits against the neighbour’s wall. Building on or near it, and digging foundations within three metres, is normally notifiable under the Party Wall Act. We flag it clearly.

Building Control packages

Calculations for the beams, columns, padstones and foundations, prepared in a format suitable for submission, so approval does not hold up your start date.

Before the builder starts

Builders price side-returns from the steel. Getting the engineer in before the builder starts means accurate quotes, no mid-job surprises and no waiting on calcs with a crew on site.

Where side meets rear: steel sized for everything above, so the new kitchen stays open underneath.

Section / 06 FAQ

Side-return extension structural FAQs.

If yours is not here, send it via the form or on WhatsApp. We read every enquiry.

Do I need a structural engineer for a side-return extension?

Yes, in practice always. A side-return infill removes or opens the flank wall of the back addition, which carries floors and roof above, and usually opens the rear wall at the same time. The steels, columns and frames that replace those walls have to be sized with calculations, and Building Control will want to see them before signing the work off.

What is a box frame, and will mine need one?

When the side and rear walls open up together, the corner of the house loses the walls that kept it stable. A box or goalpost frame, steel beams and columns designed as one system, replaces that stability so the new kitchen can stay open-plan. Whether yours needs one depends on how much wall remains; we confirm it from your drawings and say clearly which arrangement works.

Will I need a party wall agreement?

Almost certainly, yes. The side return sits against the neighbour’s property, so building on or near the boundary, working on the party wall, or excavating foundations within three metres of their structure is normally notifiable under the Party Wall Act. We flag clearly what applies so you can serve notice early and avoid delays. The notice itself is served by you or a party wall surveyor rather than by us.

My terrace is Victorian. Does the age of the house matter?

It matters, and it is our bread and butter. Victorian and Edwardian terraces have solid brick walls, shallow footings and party walls each side, so the existing structure needs checking as carefully as the new steel: what the flank wall carries, what the footings can take, and how the new loads get down to ground. That assessment is built into our package.

When should the engineer get involved?

Before the builder starts, ideally as soon as your architect’s design is settled. Builders price side-returns from the steel, so having the calculations and beam schedule ready means accurate quotes rather than provisional sums, and no crew standing idle waiting for calcs mid-job. It is the cheapest point in the project to get the structure right.

How fast can calculations be turned around?

It depends on the scope and what you already have. A straightforward side-return with good drawings is quicker than a wraparound needing a site visit first. We aim to respond to enquiries promptly and will give you a realistic timescale once we understand the project. If you are working to a builder’s start date, tell us, and we will be straight about whether it is achievable.

Do you cover South West London?

Yes. Putney, Wandsworth, Fulham, Wimbledon, Richmond, Hammersmith and the wider South West London area are part of our regular coverage, and we cover the wider London area where suitable. Send your postcode with a short description of the project and we will confirm whether we can help.
