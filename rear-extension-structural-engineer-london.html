Section / 01 When calculations help

What your rear extension needs calculations for.

Every rear extension puts new structure under old loads. These are the elements Building Control will expect to see justified.

Opening up the rear wall

The defining moment of the project: the back of the house comes out and a steel beam carries everything that used to sit on it. We size that beam and check what it lands on.

Bifold and sliding-door openings

Wide glazed openings mean long spans and small piers. We design the beam above and, where the piers are too small to brace the wall, a goalpost frame.

Goalpost frames

A steel frame of two posts and a beam that keeps the rear of the house stable when almost all the wall becomes glass. Common on modern rear extensions, and always calculated.

Double-storey rear extensions

Two storeys means bigger loads, upper floors bearing on new beams, and foundations working harder. The package grows; the logic stays the same.

Padstones and foundations

Every beam needs bearings that work and foundations that can take the load, new and existing. We check the whole load path, not just the beam.

Building Control packages

Calculations and drawings prepared in a format suitable for submission, so approval does not hold up your start date.

Builder or architect requests

If your architect’s drawings say “beam to structural engineer’s design” or your builder has asked for the calcs to price the job, that is exactly what we provide.

Section / 06 FAQ

Rear extension structural FAQs.

If yours is not here, send it via the form or on WhatsApp. We read every enquiry.

Do I need a structural engineer as well as an architect?

Usually, yes. They do different jobs. The architect designs the space: layout, light, planning approval. The structural engineer makes it stand up: sizing the beams, checking the foundations and producing the calculations Building Control requires. Architect’s drawings for a rear extension typically note “to structural engineer’s design” at every beam. That is the work we take on, in coordination with your architect.

What calculations does a rear extension need?

Typically: the beam or beams over the opened rear wall, any beams picking up upper floors or roofs, lintels over new openings, padstones and bearings, and a check that the foundations, new and existing, can carry the loads. We confirm the exact scope from your drawings before quoting, so there are no surprises.

Can you size the steel beams for my bifold opening?

Yes. Wide bifold and sliding-door openings are one of the most common things we design: the beam above, the posts or piers each side, and where the piers are too small, a goalpost frame to keep the wall stable. Tell us the rough opening width and what sits above it and we will advise what is involved.

Does a double-storey rear extension change the calculations?

Yes, but not the process. Two storeys means bigger loads: upper floors bearing on new beams, taller walls, and foundations working harder, so the beams and foundations are checked for more. The package is larger, the logic is the same, and we confirm the scope from your drawings before quoting.

Will I need a party wall agreement for a rear extension?

Often, yes, in terraced and semi-detached houses. Building along or near the boundary, or excavating new foundations within three metres of a neighbour’s structure, is usually notifiable under the Party Wall Act. We flag clearly whether your project is likely to be notifiable so you can serve notice early and avoid delays. The notice itself is served by you or a party wall surveyor rather than by us.

How fast can calculations be turned around?

It depends on the scope and what you already have. A straightforward rear opening with good drawings is quicker than a double-storey scheme needing a site visit first. We aim to respond to enquiries promptly and will give you a realistic timescale once we understand the project. If you are working to a builder’s start date, tell us, and we will be straight about whether it is achievable.

Do you cover South West London?

Yes. Putney, Wandsworth, Fulham, Wimbledon, Richmond, Hammersmith and the wider South West London area are part of our regular coverage, and we cover the wider London area where suitable. Send your postcode with a short description of the project and we will confirm whether we can help.
