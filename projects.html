Overview / Projects

The shape of the projects we typically deliver.

Most enquiries fall into a handful of project types. This page sets out what each one involves, what gets delivered, and how long the structural side typically takes. Specific project case studies will be added as clients approve them for publication - until then, we’ve kept this page general and accurate rather than dressing the practice up with names that might not represent your situation.

P / 01

Rear & wrap-around extensions

Single and double-storey rear extensions, side returns, and combined wrap-arounds. Typical structural scope: steel frames at the rear opening, padstone design, foundation strategy at the party wall and flank. End-to-end packages or structure-only.

Steel frame · Foundations · BR plans
P / 02

Loft conversions

Dormer, hip-to-gable, L-shaped and mansard conversions. Steel beams on the existing ridge or eaves, posi-joist floor design, dormer framing, and structural sign-off for Building Control.

Beams · Posi-joists · Dormer framing
P / 03

Internal wall removals

Load-bearing wall removals to open up kitchens and ground-floor living spaces. Steel beam sizing (UB / UC / PFC), padstone design, and temporary works advice. Drawings detailed enough that the steel supplier prices them without callbacks.

UB / UC / PFC · Padstones · Temp works
P / 04

Whole-house refurbishments

Internal layout reworks, new staircase positions, basement-and-ground-floor reconfigurations. Coordinated structural and architectural packages so the contractor prices a single drawing set.

Layout · Stairs · Re-servicing
P / 05

New-build & infill homes

Single-plot new builds, infill and mews developments. Foundation strategy, frame design, robustness checks, and full BR submission. Suited to small-scale developers or homeowners building one-offs.

Foundations · Frame · BR full plans
P / 06

Structural reports & surveys

Pre-purchase structural surveys, cracking and movement investigations, chimney-removal assessments, and reports on existing alterations. Concise, signed reports your solicitor or insurer can rely on. More on crack inspections →

Purchase · Cracking · Alterations
