Napier Road - wrap-around extension & full internal reconfiguration.

A Victorian terrace in Fulham with a classic narrow-side-return kitchen and a disconnected rear garden. The brief was simple - open the ground floor completely, bring natural light deep into the plan, and do it within the existing permitted development envelope wherever possible.

We delivered the full package: structural design, architectural drawings, building control submission, and on-site support through a 16-week build.

Brief

The ground floor of the property was fragmented across three small rooms: a kitchen, a dining room, and a separate side-return utility. The clients wanted a continuous living-dining-kitchen space, direct connection to the garden, and a workable plan that didn’t over-commit the budget on a large rear-wall opening.

Structural approach

The ground floor had two load-bearing walls and a chimney breast carrying the first-floor loads. Removing them cleanly required a steel frame spanning the full width of the plan, picking up both the party wall masonry and the flank wall. A 305 × 165 UB 40 was used as the primary rear beam; two supporting UC 203 columns were concealed within the new partition build-ups.

Foundations were trench fill along the new rear wall, stepped down where the rear wall tied into the existing bay. A short section of underpinning was required at the party wall where the old cellar light-well had weakened the existing footing.

Why this matters. Many rear extensions fail at the party wall interface. Designing the underpinning upfront (rather than discovering it on site) saved the clients roughly 3 weeks of programme and a significant change-order.

Architectural detail

The rear and side-return extensions were combined into a single L-shaped volume with a flat roof and a continuous slot rooflight along the party wall. Black aluminium sliding doors give the rear elevation its character while keeping the palette restrained. The side-return wall is solid to preserve privacy from the neighbours; glazing is concentrated at the rear and in the rooflight.

Approvals

The side-return element fell within permitted development limits, so we submitted a Certificate of Lawfulness rather than a full planning application. The rear extension was compliant with PD size limits, which avoided a full planning submission entirely. The building regulations submission was a full-plans application; Building Control accepted the structural package without comment, and approvals were issued five working days after submission.

On site

We attended site for the key structural sequences - pad-stones, beam lifting, and the underpinning pour - and answered the contractor’s RFIs within 24 hours throughout the build. The frame went in during week four; the envelope was watertight by week nine; the build completed on programme in week sixteen.

Outcome

The completed ground floor runs at 63 m² of continuous living space, from the front reception through to the garden. The clients report no structural issues, no settlement cracking, and a build cost that came in 2% under the original tender figure - largely because the structural package was specific enough for the contractor to price properly from day one.
